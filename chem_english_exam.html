Ninth-graders from PPMG - Vratsa demonstrated scientific skills in a binary lesson in chemistry and English

Students of IX in class and their teachers Dimitrina Milcheva (Chemistry and JSC) and Maya Genkova (English) held a binary lesson on the topic: "Chemistry in us and around us". During the lesson, the ninth-graders from PPMG - Vratsa applied the practically learned knowledge of chemistry by explaining it in English.

Four teams researched and proved the presence of various nutrients - carbohydrates, proteins, starch, and vitamins. Using chemical tests, they have successfully identified the ingredients of various foods and drinks, while demonstrating good English proficiency. Each team completed the tasks with enthusiasm and scientific approach.

Guests of the lesson were: Mrs. Annie Georgieva - Senior Expert in Natural Sciences and Ecology at the Russian Federation - Vratsa, Mrs. Rosenka Kabatliiska - Senior Foreign and Native Language Expert and the School Director Mrs. Tatiana Vasileva. They expressed their satisfaction with the commitment and hard work of the students. "These types of lessons foster critical thinking, experimental approach and teamwork, which is essential for young people's future development," the experts shared.

The binary lesson has shown that science and language skills can be complemented in an interesting and attractive way. Students not only learned more about chemical processes in and around us, they also improved their ability to present scientific information in English. Initiatives like these prove that education can be useful, fun, and inspiring at the same time.
