"STEAM - Based Teaching" and "Artificial Intelligence and Virtual Reality in Language Learning" - Participation of Teachers in Erasmus+ Program Courses

Within the framework of their professional development, the teachers from PPMG "Akad Ivan Tsenov" - Vratsa, Mrs. Veselka Valova, Deputy Director and Senior Mathematics Teacher, Mrs. Annie Pavlova, Senior English Teacher and Mrs. Pepa Pavlava, Project Coordinator and Senior Physics and Astronomy Teacher, took part in the trainings under the Erasmus+ program, targeting STEAM - based teaching methods, as well as the application of artificial intelligence (AI) and virtual reality (VR) in language learning. These international courses were held in Tallinn, Estonia in implementation of Project No. 2024-1-BG01-KA121-SCH-000202833 on Erasmus Accreditation for School Education KA-121-SCH. The training has provided a valuable opportunity to refine teaching practices, enhance digital competencies and apply innovative pedagogical approaches.

STEAM-related modules (Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics) focused on interdisciplinary, project-based learning that encourages creativity, collaboration and critical thinking among students. Teachers had the opportunity to try hands-on activities and digital tools supporting STEAM principles, as well as exchange experiences with colleagues from other European countries.

AI and VR training introduced participants to the latest technologies that transform language learning into an interactive and personalised process. Artificial intelligence tools demonstrated how adaptive systems can meet students’ individual needs and provide immediate feedback, while VR has created opportunities to practice language skills in a realistic virtual environment.

Teachers worked in teams and, alongside their peers, discussed the challenges and benefits of using the STEAM approach in education, shared their experiences and perspectives on integrating AI and VR in the education process, and explored potential strategies for introducing these technologies into the classroom. As a result of working together, the teams developed lesson plans based on the idea of using modern technology in the learning environment.

Course participants got acquainted with the specifics of the Estonian education system and visited the Ehte Humanitaargumnaasium High School, where their Estonian colleagues shared their intelligent solutions for educational innovation.

Participation in the Erasmus + courses contributed significantly to the professional growth of teachers and the modernization of the learning process in their educational institution. The knowledge and experience gained will be used to create a more engaging, inclusive and future-oriented learning environment for students. An electronic handbook for teachers, including the developed lesson plans, is about to be issued, which will be available in the school's e-library and published on the PPMG - Vratsa website.
